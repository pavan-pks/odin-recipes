Fried Rice

A fast, flavorful way to use up leftover rice!

Ingredients

  • 3 cups cooked rice (preferably day-old and chilled)
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil (divided)
  • 2 large eggs, lightly beaten
  • 1 small onion, finely diced
  • 1 cup frozen peas and carrots, thawed
  • 3 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 2 green onions, sliced (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat.
  2. Pour in the beaten eggs and scramble them quickly, breaking them into small pieces. Remove the eggs from the pan and set aside.
  3. Add the remaining tablespoon of oil to the same pan. Sauté the diced onion, peas, and carrots for about 3 to 4 minutes until tender.
  4. Turn the heat up to high, add the chilled rice to the pan, and break up any clumps with a spatula.
  5. Drizzle the soy sauce and sesame oil evenly over the rice, tossing everything together for 2 to 3 minutes until the rice is heated through and slightly crisp.
  6. Stir the scrambled eggs back into the rice mixture.
  7. Remove from heat, garnish with sliced green onions, and serve hot.
