Chicken Biryani

A classic, flavorful layered rice dish perfect for special occasions.

Ingredients

  • For the Rice:
    • 2 cups Basmati rice (washed and soaked for 30 minutes)
    • 4 cups water
    • 2 whole cardamoms
    • 1 bay leaf
    • 1 tsp salt
  • For the Chicken Marinade:
    • 500g chicken, bone-in cut into pieces
    • 1/2 cup plain yogurt
    • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
    • 1 tsp red chili powder
    • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
    • 1 tsp biryani masala powder
  • For Layering & Cooking:
    • 2 large onions, thinly sliced and fried until golden (Barista)
    • 3 tbsp ghee or vegetable oil
    • 1/4 cup fresh mint leaves, chopped
    • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
    • A pinch of saffron soaked in 2 tbsp warm milk

Instructions

  1. Mix all the marinade ingredients with the chicken and let it rest in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Boil the water with the whole spices and salt. Add the soaked Basmati rice and cook until it is 70% done (still has a slight bite to it). Drain and set aside.
  3. Heat ghee or oil in a heavy-bottomed pot. Add the marinated chicken and cook over medium heat for 10 to 12 minutes until the chicken is mostly cooked through and a thick gravy forms.
  4. Layer the cooked rice evenly on top of the chicken curry layer in the pot.
  5. Top the rice layer with the fried onions, chopped mint, cilantro, and drizzle the saffron milk over the top for color and aroma.
  6. Seal the pot tightly with a lid (or use aluminum foil before placing the lid) to trap the steam.
  7. Cook on very low heat (Dum style) for 15 to 20 minutes until the rice is fully tender and fluffy. Gently fluff and mix before serving.
