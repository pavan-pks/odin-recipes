Classic Homemade Vanilla Ice Cream

A rich, creamy no-churn recipe that doesn't require an expensive machine!

Ingredients

  • Ice Cream Base:
    • 2 cups heavy whipping cream (cold)
    • 1 can (14 ounces) sweetened condensed milk (chilled)
    • 1 tbsp pure vanilla extract (or vanilla bean paste)
    • A tiny pinch of fine salt
  • Optional Mix-ins:
    • 1/2 cup chocolate chips or crushed cookies
    • 1/4 cup swirl of caramel or fudge sauce

Instructions

  • In a large bowl, pour in the cold heavy whipping cream. Use a hand mixer or stand mixer to whip the cream on medium-high speed until stiff peaks form.
  • In a separate large bowl, whisk together the sweetened condensed milk, vanilla extract, and a pinch of salt until smooth.
  • Gently fold a large spoonful of the whipped cream into the condensed milk mixture to lighten it up.
  • Add the rest of the whipped cream and gently fold it in with a rubber spatula until completely combined. (Be careful not to over-mix, or you will lose the trapped air!)
  • If you are using any mix-ins like chocolate chips or cookie chunks, gently fold them in during this step.
  • Pour the liquid ice cream mixture into a 9x5-inch loaf pan or an airtight, freezer-safe container. Smooth the top with your spatula.
  • If desired, drizzle caramel or fudge sauce over the top and use a butter knife to swirl it into the mixture.
  • Cover the container tightly with plastic wrap (touching the surface of the cream to prevent ice crystals) or a lid. Freeze for at least 6 hours, or overnight, until firm.
